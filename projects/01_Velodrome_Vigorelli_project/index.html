Velodromo Vigorelli

Academic Project – Redevelopment of the Vigorelli Velodrome, Milan IUAV University of Venice, 2013–2014 | Supervisor: Prof. Angelo Villa
The redevelopment project for the Maspes-Vigorelli Velodrome seeks to restore a key piece of Milan’s architectural and sporting heritage by transforming it into a contemporary, multifunctional facility open to the city year-round. Located in the north-western quadrant of Milan, near the historic Trade Fair district, the intervention reimagines the Vigorelli as a vibrant public space dedicated to sport, culture, and community life. The proposal retains and restores the iconic wooden cycling track while redefining the central field as a flexible space capable of hosting sporting events, concerts, exhibitions, and public gatherings. New underground volumes are introduced beneath the curved ends of the track, accommodating a gym and an exhibition hall, and allowing for a seamless connection between the interior and surrounding public spaces—blurring the boundary between stadium and urban park. A new lightweight polycarbonate roof spans the entire facility, including the grandstands and central field, ensuring both protection and transparency while showcasing the original structure. The under-stand areas are reconfigured to include amenities such as shops, cafés, a two-level gym, locker rooms, and administrative offices.The design pays particular attention to the relationship between the building and its context, enhancing pedestrian accessibility, creating green spaces, and extending the architectural rhythm of the façade into the surrounding paving. The project offers a forward-looking model of adaptive reuse, combining historical preservation with spatial innovation to reestablish the Vigorelli as a civic landmark for the city of Milan.
